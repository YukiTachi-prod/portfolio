Reflection

Honestly, I didn't expect much from this activity at first. I thought it was just going to be another requirement, show up, plant a tree, take a photo, and go home. But it turned out to be way more meaningful than I anticipated. We planted trees along the riverbank, and even just being there made me realize how much that area needed it. The soil near the river was already showing signs of erosion, and the water didn't look as clean as it probably should. It made me think about how easy it is to overlook environmental problems when you're not physically standing in front of them. The actual planting was harder than I expected. Digging into the ground, making sure the roots had enough space, and patting the soil down properly was simple work, but it felt oddly satisfying. There's something about putting something into the ground and knowing it's going to grow long after you're gone that hits differently. What struck me the most was thinking about the long-term impact. These trees won't fully mature for a very long time, but they'll help prevent soil erosion, filter the water, and give shade to the riverbank for future generations. That kind of contribution feels rare because most things we do give immediate results, but this one requires patience. This activity reminded me that community service doesn't have to be grand to be meaningful. Sometimes, all it takes is showing up and planting one tree.

— Gutierrez
